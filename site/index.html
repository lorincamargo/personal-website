About


Lorin Camargo was born and raised in San José, California. She is a graduate of San Francisco State University, where she studied Apparel Design and Merchandising. Following graduation, Lorin lived in New York, where she worked in fashion production, and as a freelance seamstress and sewing instructor. Lorin realized an interest in community building after taking part in a Civic Hackathon which aimed to solve community issues in Brooklyn, NY.

After moving back to the Bay Area, Lorin has worked as a Council Assistant for Council District 3, the Downtown core, of San José. Lorin has also been an active leader in volunteer-run organizations Code for San José and Catalyze SV, both of which work toward civic improvement through innovation and community engagement.

Lorin is currently based in Melbourne, Australia with a focus on civic design and technology.

Public Art Grant-athon

Code for San José (CfSJ) is a volunteer organization made up of makers, designers, developers, and subject matter experts who come together to use technology to solve civic problems.


In December of 2017, with the help of fellow volunteers, Lorin organized and facilitated CfSJ’s first ever Public Art Grant-athon. The idea was to combine the need for more public art in San José with the style of a hackathon. In short, it was a day-long opportunity for members from the community to meet, form teams and write grant proposals with the hope to add some artwork to public space in San José.


Connections were made at the event, including a team of two made up of a software engineer and a local artist who went on to submit a grant proposal to the Burning Man Global Art Grants Committee. The project idea is to create a more interactive experience at public concerts in San Jose by collaborating with the City and other local organizations. We hope to see public art from this team and from other members of CfSJ around the city very soon!

SJD3 Website

Council District 3 is San José’s downtown district, encompassing a high density area which is home to a variety of people from different social and economic backgrounds. The vision for the District 3 Council Office under Councilmember Raul Peralez is to make San José a city where everyone can afford to live, find dignified work and play in peace.


As a Council Aide for the City of San José, Lorin designed and built a website for the District 3 Council Office.


One goal for the website was to make it easier for the public to search for and find specific policy memos. This was achieved by providing an interface that split up memoranda by various categories (i.e. Housing & Renters’ Rights, Economic Development, or Arts & Culture). This search function is not available at the official City’s website.


It was important to the Office that this website would act as a resource for San José residents. For one example, the website includes contact information for upwards of 50 Neighborhood Association leaders as well as for Council Staff who act as Liaisons, to make it easy for community members to become involved and to report concerns in a particular neighborhood.


Lorin worked directly with the Councilmember and fellow staff members, as well as with the community to receive feedback on the app and make changes accordingly. It was important that the website would be simple and easy for all ages to use, and it was key that the site would be responsive on all devices as well as easy to maintain in the future for non-developers working in the office.


At the completion of the website, Lorin created a Website Maintenance Guidebook to ensure future District 3 Council Staff are able to quickly and easily maintain the website without the presence of a developer.

Renters' Rights Guide

Code for San José (CfSJ) is a volunteer organization made up of makers, designers, developers, and subject matter experts who come together to use technology to solve civic problems.


As a member of CfSJ, Lorin has been involved with the design of a new web and mobile app entitled Renters’ Rights Guide (RRG). RRG is a tool that was created to inform renters about the laws and programs in place to protect them from unfair practices.

Pop-up Bikeways Promo Video

Pop-up Bikeways was a one-week demonstration of protected bikeways put on by the City of San José’s Department of Transportation. The installation was made to help gather feedback on new infrastructure and was part of a larger planning process to build a better bicycling network in and around Downtown San José.


As a member of Code for San José, Lorin co-created a promotional video for the Pop-up Bikeways project to help the City share this installation with the public. The video was shown at community events, on social media and on the City’s website.

Project 6

The mission at Hack the Patriarchy is to create innovative solutions for marginalized communities around the world. As a participant at San José’s first Hack the Patriarchy event, Lorin worked within a team of five to create a prototype game that is geared toward educating young females about common scenarios that women face in the workplace. And, hopefully, offer good advice as to how to navigate these situations.


Lorin worked with the team to create the storyline for the game and was also the Designer and Illustrator for the team. She created graphic illustrations for the game, which included a main character, side characters and scenery.

Project 5

The goal of Hack Red Hook is to bring developers, artists, community organizers, and builders to the same table to work together to cultivate neighborhood pride. As a participant of Hack Red Hook 2016, Lorin worked with a team of six to create an interactive lighting system powered by solar panels which was built to illuminate neighborhood sidewalks. The neighborhood, to the dismay of the residents, was lit by diesel-generated flood lights which polluted the air and disrupted sleep for much of the community.


With the team, Lorin met and spoke with community members as well as local police officers about the current lighting, which was under the control of the NYPD. She also helped design and build the prototype, as well as co-designed the logo and branding for the project.
